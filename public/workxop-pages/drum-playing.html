Traditional Drum Playing Workshop

The Traditional Drum Playing Workshop provides an in-depth exploration of Sri Lanka's drumming traditions, emphasizing their cultural and historical significance. Drumming in Sri Lanka serves not only as a musical expression but also as a spiritual practice that reflects the country’s heritage and connection to nature. This workshop offers participants the opportunity to understand and engage with the role of drumming in Sri Lankan culture.

Participants will learn about the history of drumming in Sri Lanka, which dates back to ancient times when rhythms were used for communication, ceremonies, and storytelling. The workshop includes practical sessions where attendees can experience traditional drumming techniques and discover how this art form has evolved into a practice with therapeutic and meditative benefits.

Highlights:

  • Learn how drumming evolved from ancient communication tools to cultural symbols, deeply intertwined with Sri Lankan rituals, festivals, and daily life.
  • Discover the unique stories and sounds of key traditional drums, including:
    • Geta Bera: Revered in Kandyan dance, symbolizing grace and strength.
    • Thammattama: A two-sided drum used in ceremonies and temple rituals.
    • Yak Bera: Known for its pivotal role in devil dance performances and healing ceremonies.
  • Learn about the therapeutic aspects of rhythm and its role in promoting mindfulness, emotional balance, and spiritual connection.
  • Understand the effects of synchronized drumming, which supports a sense of unity and flow.
  • Practice rhythms inspired by Sri Lankan folklore, exploring the drum's role in rituals, appreciation of nature, and cultural celebrations.
  • Participate in group drumming sessions to experience its ability to encourage collaboration and a sense of shared purpose.
